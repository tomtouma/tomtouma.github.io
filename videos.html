In Elma, Washington, our NASA JPL/Caltech team live tested our advanced 'autonomy solution' called NeBula which can be physically augmented to Boston Dynamics' Spot quadruped. NeBula provides robotic platforms with high level autonomous capabilities. This is the most advanced autonomous system with which Boston Dynamics' Spot robot or any quadruped has ever been endowed. (BACKUP)



Our NASA/JPL/Caltech team is participating in the DARPA Subterranean Challenge to develop fully autonomous UGV's, UAV's, Tanks, Quadrupeds and Hybrid systems to explore subsurface voids with a dual focus on planetary exploration and terrestrial applications in search and rescue, mining industry, and advanced AI/Autonomy in extreme environments. To date, we have placed 2nd (Aug 2019) and 1st (Feb 2020) in two DARPA Challenges. (BACKUP)



This took place at the Urban Circuit of the DARPA Subterranean Challenge (Feb 2020). It was a live competition and evaluation of our NeBula autonomy solution for our team of robots. Here you can see NeBula integrated on Boston Dynamics' Spot quadruped. We were able to descend multiple flights of stairs with our unique autonomy software and hardware. No other competitor was able to match this feat. This ability meant that we were able to explore farther and deeper than any other competitor, into the Satsop Nuclear Power Plant.



NASA officially presented the new '2020 Mars Rover: Perseverance' in March of 2020. In this release (2:13 minute mark), NASA discusses how the cutting edge technology that my team developed will enable autonomous robotic navigation of extreme, underground environments. NASA goes on to mention that this state-of-the-art technology could one day be used to map out caves on the Moon and Mars in preparation for the arrival of human explorers on these celestial bodies. My team and I have submitted another proposal for a mission just like this! (BACKUP)



We are the first team in the history of NASA, JPL and Caltech to win the prestigious DARPA Challenge. This is the same Department of Defense funded DARPA Challenge that led to the creation of the first stealth attack aircraft (F-117 Nighthawk), the first advanced legged robotic technology (Boston Dynamics/JPL Big Dog) and modern autonomous car technology. We are pushing the cutting edge in autonomous, unknown, underground, extreme environment exploration. (BACKUP)



Is there life beyond Earth? Scientists believe that planetary caves and subsurface voids are the most likely places for alien life to be hiding. But these environments are incredibly dangerous for humans to explore. High-level autonomy is the solution to this challenge! Our NASA JPL led team CoSTAR has developed an autonomy and AI framework for extreme environment exploration, called NeBula. I am proud to say that I am part of the development of NeBula! (BACKUP)
