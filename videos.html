Our NASA JPL/Caltech team live tests advanced 'autonomy solution' called NeBula which can be physically augmented to Boston Dynamics' Spot quadruped. NeBula provides robotic platforms with high level autonomous behaviour. This is the most advanced autonomous system with which Boston Dynamics' Spot or nay quadruped has ever been endowed. Our NASA JPL/Caltech team is called CoSTAR! We are participating in the DARPA Subterranean Challenge to develop fully autonomous UGV, UAV, Tank, Quadrupedal and Hybrid systems to explore subsurface voids with a dual focus on planetary exploration and terrestrial applications in search and rescue, mining industry, and advanced AI/Autonomy in extreme environments. (BACKUP)



This took place at the Urban Circuit (Feb 2020) of the DARPA Subterranean Challenge. It was a live competition and evaluation of our NeBula autonomy solution for our team of robots. Here you can see NeBula integrated on Boston Dynamics' Spot quadruped. We were able to descend multiple flights of stairs with our unique autonomy software and hardware. No other competitor was able to match this feat. This ability meant that we could also explore the Satsop Nuclear plant farther than any other competitor.
